New York

Manhattan

In Manhattan, you'll find 90% of New York City's attractions. The area of Manhattan known as Midtown, north of 31st Street, is where the borough's energy truly comes alive. In this area, you'll find the iconic Empire State Building, the Rockefeller Center complex, the stunning Chrysler Building, the fascinating Times Square, the renowned MoMA, and many other must-see attractions in New York.

This American city is a true street food landmark, and in Manhattan you can eat on almost every corner 24 hours a day. The best thing about these street food stands is that you can try specialties from all around the world and eat cheaply in New York. If you're into international cuisine, you have to visit the restaurants in Brooklyn or Harlem.

Skyscrapers in New York City are an essential part of both its daily life and its historical identity. Some of the city's most stunning postcards are those that capture the silhouette of its tall buildings, illuminated by hundreds of lights, standing along the Hudson River and reaching toward the night sky.
